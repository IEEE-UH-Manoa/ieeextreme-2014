About IEEEXtreme

IEEEXtreme is a global challenge in which teams of IEEE Student members, supported by an IEEE Student Branch, advised and proctored by an IEEE member, compete in a 24-hour time span against each other to solve a set of programming problems.

The IEEE Student Branch at UH Manoa hosts this international competition for local students annually, and has done so for many years now. Join us for an exciting 24 hours of comradery, free food and awesome code. If you don't know how to program, or you're not that good of a programmer, get a team, a name and compete anyways!!!

Its a great opportunity to hang out with our peers in the IEEE club room, eat some free food and bust out some cool code. One of the fondest memories our alumni walk away with is of the IEEEXtreme programming competition.

Requirements

You do need to be a student member of the IEEE Professional Society, but that comes with
many perks also associated with our local branch! After that, as along as you're a student, you're allowed to compete.

If you're not already a student member of the IEEE Professional Society, sign up here: http://www.ieee.org/membership_services/membership/join/index.html

How-To

1. Grab your friends, form a group and think of a name
2. Email ieee@hawaii.edu so we can give you Tep's proctor number to signup
3. Gather your team's information, including everyone's IEEE number
4. Signup at http://xtreme.vtools.ieee.org/
5. Prepare and show up on the competition day!
